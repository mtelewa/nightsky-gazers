  • NamibRand - Namibia

Nestled within the Namibian Desert, holds the distinction of being the inaugural recipient of Dark Sky certification as an international reserve boasting the darkest skies in Africa.

  • Merzouga - Italy

The Sahara Desert, along with the moon, stars, planets, constellations, meteors, and the Milky Way, has captivated humans for millennia. The Sahara is a prominent attraction on any journey to Morocco, and stargazing in its desert expanse promises an unforgettable experience. This is yet another compelling reason not to overlook it while in the region!

  • Siwa Oasis - Egypt

Located deep within the Western Desert, Siwa Oasis is among Egypt's most secluded and peaceful destinations. Renowned for its stunning night skies, limited light pollution, and sparse human activity, Siwa Oasis offers an excellent opportunity for stargazing and enjoying the majestic dunes.

  • Westhavelland Nature Park - Germany

Stands as the country's inaugural Dark Sky Park, offering unparalleled opportunities for a celestial exploration. Situated just a straightforward two-hour drive west of Berlin, it serves as an excellent addition to a city getaway. Recognized as an International Dark Sky Reserve since 2014, the park boasts its darkest skies between the quaint towns of Gülpe and Nennhausen, both boasting minimal light pollution.

  • Tuscany - Italy

Renowned as the birthplace of the esteemed astronomer Galileo, stands out as an ideal destination for stargazing in Italy. With its clear skies and minimal light pollution, the region's picturesque landscapes of rolling hills, rustic farms, and hilltop villages provide an enchanting backdrop for observing the celestial wonders.

  • Haunte-Pyrénées - France

A segment of Europe's largest International Dark Sky Reserve. Positioned at an elevation of nearly 10,000 feet, the 19th-century Pic du Midi observatory ranks among Europe's premier stargazing destinations. Easily reachable via cable car, this site boasts the impressive Gentilli dome telescope, initially deployed by NASA in the 1960s to chart the moon ahead of the Apollo missions. Additionally, it hosts Europe's highest planetarium within the historic Baillaud dome.

  • Atacama Desert - Chile

The remarkable clarity of the Atacama Desert offers unobstructed vistas of the Southern Hemisphere's renowned constellations, such as the Tarantula Nebula, the Fornax Cluster of galaxies, the Southern Cross, and even the Large Magellanic Cloud, a satellite galaxy of the Milky Way. These exceptional features have led many to regard Chile's Atacama Desert as the ultimate destination for stargazing on a global scale.

  • Alabama Hills - California (USA)

An extraordinary setting to marvel at the splendor of the night sky. Situated in a remote area with minimal light pollution, it provides optimal conditions for stargazing. The distinctive rock formations in this region also serve as a stunning backdrop for astrophotography, attracting both amateur and professional astronomers to this dream destination.

  • Natural Bridges - Utah (USA)

The primary allure of the dark skies in this area is the captivating "river of light" effect produced by the Milky Way as it ascends above the Owachomo Bridge, a natural rock formation. This bridge acts as a unique aperture to the night sky, elegantly encasing the myriad stars visible to the naked eye. For an immersive experience, consider camping overnight to fully appreciate the spectacle.

  • Iriomote-Ishigaki National Park - Japan

Situated within Japan's Okinawa Prefecture, this was the inaugural location in Japan to be granted accreditation as an International Dark Sky Place. Positioned on the Yaeyama Islands near the Tropic of Cancer, it offers a vista of up to 84 of the 88 constellations officially recognized by the International Astronomical Union. Nonetheless, the clarity of stargazing varies based on seasonal and weather fluctuations.

  • Koh Kood - Thailand

Exceptionally clear skies, far removed from the light pollution of the mainland. Our advanced Observatory provides panoramic views of the star-filled sky in all directions. Embark on a journey through the Milky Way alongside our resident Astronomer, who will guide you in identifying constellations, counting Jupiter's moons, and admiring the elegant rings of Saturn.

  • Mount Bromo - Indonesia

The allure of stargazing in the Mount Bromo area lies in its subtle beauty, which may not be readily visible to the naked eye. To witness this natural spectacle, one must utilize a DSLR camera or binoculars. This phenomenon is observable in the vicinity of Mount Bromo due to the minimal light pollution present in the area.
